Chicken Curry Fried Rice

This Chicken Curry Fried Rice is a flavorful and satisfying meal, perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

For the Garlic Curry Chicken:

  • 350g Chicken Thigh, skinless and boneless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 medium Onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp Chili Powder (or more, to taste)
  • 1 tsp Curry Powder
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp All-Purpose Flour (for coating)
  • 1 tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 10g Butter
  • Fresh Parsley, chopped (for garnish - optional)

For the Curry Fried Rice:

  • 300g Cooked Rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 tbsp Curry Powder
  • 1/2 tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • 2-3 tbsp Milk (optional, for softening)

Instructions:

Prepare the Garlic Curry Chicken:

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces, minced garlic, chili powder, 1tsp curry powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the chicken is evenly coated.
  2. Lightly dust the chicken pieces with all-purpose flour.
  3. Heat cooking oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium-low heat.
  4. Add the chicken pieces to the hot pan, ensuring not to overcrowd. Cover the pan with a lid.
  5. Cook until golden brown and crispy on the bottom, then flip and cook the other side.
  6. Add 10g of butter.
  7. Once the chicken is cooked through and crispy, remove it from the pan and set aside.
  8. In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt. Saut√© until softened and caramelized, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the onions and set aside.

Prepare the Curry Fried Rice:

  1. In the same pan or another large skillet, add 2 tbsp curry powder and heat over medium heat for 30 seconds or until fragrant, be careful not to burn the spice.
  2. Add the cooked rice and oyster sauce to the pan.
  3. Use a spatula or wooden spoon to break up the rice and mix everything thoroughly.
  4. If the rice seems too dry, add milk, a tablespoon at a time, until it reaches your desired consistency.

Assemble and Serve:

  1. Transfer the curry fried rice to a plate, you can form a dome shape to make it more presentable.
  2. Arrange the caramelized onions on one side of the rice.
  3. Slice the cooked garlic curry chicken into smaller pieces.
  4. Place the sliced chicken on top of the onions.
  5. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley (optional).
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips:

  • For the fried rice you can use cold or leftover rice that's been sitting in the fridge
  • If you don't have chili powder, a pinch of red pepper flakes will also do
